About

My name is Isaac Williams, and I'm the Data Visualization Librarian at the University of Texas at Arlington. I teach students and faculty how to use data visualization and digital humanities tools and methods, including Tableau, data cleaning, text analysis, and more, with an emphasis on critical data literacy. I'm invested in making digital research accessible to students from non-STEM backgrounds, which is partially what led me to become a certified instructor with The Carpentries in 2019.

Before my current role, I received an MLIS and a BA (in English) from UCLA. I was also a student at Riverside Community College in Riverside, California. In graduate school, I worked at the UCLA Digital Library Program and the Center for the Study of Political Graphics, as well as teaching undergraduate students and managing digital archives at the UCLA History Department.

The last book I read was "Severance" by Ling Ma.
